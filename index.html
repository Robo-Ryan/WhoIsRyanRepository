Design Process


Jobs to be Done

“People don't want a quarter-inch drill, they want a quarter inch hole.” The JTBD theory establishes that users care about the problem they have more than the solution a product offers. I use the JTBD theory in my design process to understand user journeys and generate simple solutions.

Contextual Inquiry

In my design process, I use contextual inquiry to be physically present with users while they accomplish a task, rather than asking users how they completed something after the fact. Including software teams early and bringing users into the conversation fosters a deeper understanding of user requirements, leading to more effective and user-centered solutions.

Latch: As a Product Manager of Latch's core product offering, a smart lock, I built software to support resident journeys of unlocking their home doors 4.5x/day and while also reducing property management operating expenses. The solution space was constrained by limitations of battery powered devices- every feature released had a tradeoff power, which made every product decision metrics-informed.

Roles: Associate Product Manager / Product Manager
Technologies and Tools: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Sketch, Mixpanel, Figma, Airtable, JIRA

Google Nest x Latch Integration: As Product Owner, I worked with Google Nest’s product team to establish an API authentication between our backends, which gave Latch property managers enterprise-wide authority over Nest products. This helped property managers control Nest thermostats remotely after resident move-out (and make sure airconditioning wasn’t left on!). I ran 4 pilots that expanded to 40 buildings. Success depended on sharing goals across software teams and customer success empowerment at launch. We were able to include the new feature in our Latch offering and owe particular success through regular bug monitoring with our ops team, insight tracking in airtable, and stakeholder management at pilot sites.

Roles: Product Manager / Product Owner.
Technologies and Tools: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Sketch, Mixpanel, Figma, Airtable, JIRA
